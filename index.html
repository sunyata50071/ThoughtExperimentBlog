Does AI truly understand what it is doing?

Imagine a person who only speaks English is locked in a room with Egyptian hieroglyphs. Boxes of English instructions are passed into the room for matching hieroglyphs with hieroglyphs. The instructions say, for example, "take this symbol pictured and put it next to this other symbol". As instructions come in, the person in the box places hieroglyphic answers through an outside window. Those outside the box think the person inside is fluent in hieroglyphs but in actual fact they only know English and follow instructions without knowing Egyptian hieroglyphs at all. Is AI any different?

Ibn Sina's floating man

Imagine a person were created fully formed but floating in the air. They cannot see anything and cannot feel their body. They cannot even feel the air. Would this person affirm the existence of themselves? Would the floating man think he exists when he doesn't experience through his five senses?

Mary's room

Mary has mastered complete scientific knowledge of the physical world but lives in a black and white world. She has learnt from books all the physical properties of colour but has never experienced it. One day she stumbles upon a red door. Upon experiencing colour for the first time does she aquire new knowledge?

The Ship of Theseus

Every year the Athenians sailed to Delos in honour of the God Apollo. Over time the ship has its parts replaced. Imagine that the old parts that were replaced are kept and used to build a ship. Eventually, after the original ship has all of its parts replaced, there's two ships. Which is the ship of Theseus?
